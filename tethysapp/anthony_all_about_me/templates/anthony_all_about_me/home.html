Anthony: All About Me

Birthplace: Arequipa, Arequipa, Peru

Arequipa is such a great city. I think some of the best memories of my childhood happened here. I lived here until I was 8 years old. I remember always watching the Misti Volcano from my bedroom window and wishing to be closer. Hiking up there is still on my bucket list.


This is the house where I grew up. It seemed so big for me when I was a kid.


This is the house where my father grew up. My uncles, aunts and cousins still live nearby. I remember that I was in this house when an earthquake happened on Jun 23rd, 2001


I personally love the food from Arequipa, Peru. In the following image you can see from left to right: ocopa arequipena, rocoto relleno, chupe de camarones and adobo arequipeno


This is the Arequipa Peru LDS Temple, I would love to be there for the dedication in Nov 2019, but I will be in the middle of Fall Semester.

Trujillo Peru Childhood

I moved to Trujillo when I was 8 years old because of my father's job. This was my house when I was living there.


This is the Trujillo Peru LDS Temple. I was able to be in the dedication and currently it is my favorite temple.

Ica Peru Youth

I moved to Ica when I was 10 years old because of my father's job. This was my house when I was living there.


In August 15th, 2007 at 6:42PM (Peruvian Time) I experienced an earthquake in this city. These are some images after the earthquake.

Lima Peru Youth

I moved to Lima when I was 14 years old and I finished my High School here.


I lived with my family in Lima for 3 years in this house.


I lived with my Grandparents in Lima for almost a year in this house because my family moved to a Chimbote in Northern Peru. These were good times.


I studied in the "Universidad Nacional de Ingenieria" for a semester before getting ready to go on a mission.

Chimbote Preparation for Mission

I moved to Chimbote with my parents before going on a mission to this house.


I loved the food in Chimbote. This restaurant has such a great ceviche. If you visit this city anytime, you should definitely go and eat there.

Mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

I was called to serve in the Argentina Posadas Mission. This mission was unique because it had 2 countries in its territory (Paraguay and Argentina). In the paraguayan region they spoke guarani and spanish and it had such a hot and rainy tropical weather. It was hard to be a missionary there, but it was so worth it. I got to meet really good people and I loved the place. The Argentinian region had the Iguazu falls inside the mission borders. I was able to go there once. It was awesome!!

Life in Lima after my mission

I returned to Lima after my mission and started studying in the Pontific Catholic University of Peru for a year befoe transferring to BYU.


I lived in an apartment in this building by myself while studying in Lima. It was the first time that I lived alone. It was great!!!
